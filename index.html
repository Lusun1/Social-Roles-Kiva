Documentation on social role validation
This documenation introduced the social roles process on Kiva in the CSCW paper Section 6.4.1 and Section 6.4.2. The survery forms below show the example of our user studies on Amazon MTurk.
  • Instruction to name social roles: This survey introduced how to name social roles. We set this survey as a qualification test for MTurk workers, which means only the workers passed this test could contribute on the social roles naming task(corresponding to Section 6.4.1 in the paper).
  • Name social roles: This survery collected workers' answers on the name of social roles(corresponding to Section 6.4.1 in the paper).
  • Intruder task: This survery collected workers' answers on the intruder task. (corresponding to Section 6.4.2 in the paper).
Instruction to name social roles

Introduction

This task is to name a group of users in an onling platform "Kiva.org". Kiva.org is a non-profit peer-to-peer lending platform that allows people to lend money via the Internet to low-income entrepreneurs and students all over the world. Kiva had lending teams, through which lenders who have something in common(eg: interests, occupations, religious) can band together and try to convince each other to contribute loans. Lenders could join in the team discussion to convince other members to lend and communicate with others, which is similar as the facebook group. For example: one people recommended to other members loans by sending the message: "Please consider this loan. His time will soon run out. http://www.kiva.org/lend/524358".
The goal of this task to provide the name of a group of team members. For example, we could name the user who always introduce different loans to team members, as 'persuader'; we could name the user who always talk to different people as 'communicator'. In this study, we will provide some description of a group of users and some texts which these group members said in the team discussion. Your task is to use several key words to describe these roles based on these descriptions and their messages. Please use the description as the main basis when you name the group of users because it is the common description for the whole group members, and then use the text just for evaluation.

Explanation of social roles:
Individualists: people who are lack of engagement and inactive in the team. They focus on their personal goals instead of contributing to the team. For example, they send surveys whch are unrelated to the team, ask for favors on their personal tasks or do advertisements unrelated to Kiva in the team discussion.
Welcomers:people who welcome to new members or respond to them after they first post on Kiva. These active and inclusive members also provide supportive encouragement.
Networkers:people who seems to be the network hubs and frequently interact with others. They are active engagers who are talktive and send long, detailed messages to persuader others to lend, provide detailed information about loans or coordinate others team members. They keep open and positive attitude in the team discussion.
